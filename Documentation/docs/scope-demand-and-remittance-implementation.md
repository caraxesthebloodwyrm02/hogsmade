# Scope demand and remittance contracts — implementation

Deliverable from the plan: synthesized scopes, steps per scope, and a single start-to-complete flow.  
**Reference:** Bangladesh direct-beneficiary revenue work; Remittance = highest market demand; work-payment contracts via Remittance.

---

## 1. Synthesized scopes (from the whole plan)

The project has **five direct-beneficiary scopes**. One is highest in market demand; the rest are ordered by scale in your data.

| Scope                                    | Approx. scale         | Defining moment                                | Priority by demand |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| **Remittance**                           | $17.17 B (H1 FY25-26) | FX credited to you (bank/MFS)                  | **1 — highest**    |
| RMG / Export                             | $9.97 B (Jul–Sep)     | LC/FX received by you                          | 2                  |
| FDI                                      | $1.41 B (Jan–Sep)     | Equity/reinvested earnings booked to your firm | 3                  |
| E‑commerce (card)                        | Tk 21.93 cr (Aug)     | Transaction settled to you                     | 4                  |
| Domestic (retail, services, real estate) | Not quantified        | Invoice paid / COD / deed or advance to you    | 5                  |

**Builder traits** (who operates in the highest-demand scope): beneficiary-centric, settlement-moment focus, formal-channel trust, cross-border orientation, simple communication.

**Entities for remittance work-payment contracts:** Diaspora senders, international clients of BD freelancers, remote employers using BD talent, BD freelancers/contractors, platforms with BD payout, banks/MFS, EOR/payroll providers.

## 2. Steps to successfully accomplish each chunk of scope

### Scope 1: Remittance (highest demand)

1. **Choose role:** Payer (send work payment to BD), payee (receive in BD), or intermediary (platform/bank/MFS).
2. **Use formal channel:** Bank or MFS (bKash, Nagad, etc.) so the “defining moment” (credit to account) is clear and traceable.
3. **Align with policy:** Use channels that qualify for any incentive (e.g. 2.5%) and comply with local rules.
4. **Define the contract:** Agree with the other party: work delivered ↔ payment sent via remittance; settlement = when FX is credited to beneficiary account.
5. **Document and track:** Record send date, amount, beneficiary, and confirmation of credit so both sides agree “revenue happened.”

### Scope 2: Export (RMG / goods)

1. **Secure order:** Buyer (EU/US/UK/Canada or other) commits to order; terms and LC or payment method agreed.
2. **Produce and ship:** Fulfill order; ship and hand over documents per contract.
3. **Realize proceeds:** LC paid or FX received by you (exporter); this is the defining moment.
4. **Record and reconcile:** Book export revenue when proceeds are received; keep proof for compliance and tax.

### Scope 3: FDI

1. **Identify investor or reinvestment source:** Foreign entity or existing operation planning to inject capital or reinvest earnings.
2. **Structure the deal:** Equity, loan, or reinvested earnings; terms and reporting agreed.
3. **Execute and book:** Transfer or booking of funds; defining moment = amount recorded to your firm (e.g. by central bank / auditor).
4. **Comply and report:** Meet any regulatory or reporting requirements for FDI in BD.

### Scope 4: E‑commerce (card / digital)

1. **Set up merchant/payout:** List on platform or accept card/MFS; ensure payout is to your account.
2. **Sell and fulfill:** Customer pays (card/MFS or COD); for card/MFS, defining moment = transaction settled to you.
3. **Reconcile:** Match settlements to orders; treat “settled” as revenue recognized.
4. **Scale:** Optimize for volume and repeat; keep payment and delivery clear for disputes.

### Scope 5: Domestic (retail, services, real estate)

1. **Define offer:** Product, service, or property; price and terms (cash, COD, advance, installment).
2. **Deliver and get paid:** Defining moment = customer pays (cash/COD) or pays advance/signs deed (real estate).
3. **Record:** Book turnover when payment is received; keep simple records for tax and disputes.
4. **Repeat:** Build repeat custom or referrals; optional: add card/MFS for traceability.

## 3. Single start-to-complete flow (merged)

One path from start to “plan implemented” using the scopes and remittance focus.

**Start**

1. **Pick your primary scope.**
   - Default for “highest market demand” = **Remittance.**
   - If you are payee: you receive work payment via remittance. If payer: you send work payment to BD via remittance. If intermediary: you enable or formalize such contracts.

2. **Confirm your role and counterparty.**
   - Payer: diaspora employer, international client, remote company.
   - Payee: BD freelancer, contractor, vendor.
   - Intermediary: bank/MFS, platform, EOR.

3. **Use the steps for that scope (Section 2).**
   - For Remittance: steps 1–5 under “Scope 1: Remittance.”
   - For other scopes: follow the corresponding 4-step block.

4. **Define the work-payment contract.**
   - In writing or platform terms: what work, what payment, which channel (for Remittance: bank/MFS), and when revenue is recognized (for Remittance: when FX is credited to beneficiary).

5. **Execute and record.**
   - Do the work; send or receive payment; record the defining moment (e.g. “FX credited on [date]” or “LC received on [date]”).

6. **Reconcile and repeat.**
   - Match payments to work/orders; treat “credited” or “received” as revenue. Use the same flow for the next contract.

**Complete**

- You have: (a) chosen a scope (Remittance or other), (b) identified role and entities, (c) followed the steps for that scope, (d) a clear contract that ties work to payment and defines the moment revenue is recognized, and (e) at least one cycle executed and recorded.
- For Remittance specifically: at least one work-payment flow where settlement = money sent from abroad and credited to a BD account (bank/MFS), with both sides agreeing that “revenue happened” at that moment.
