### Display Rate vs. Proposal Rate Split

**Display rate**: $25/hr (competitive entry, maximum search visibility)
**Proposal rate**: $30/hr (adjusted per proposal, within Upwork's per-engagement flexibility)

- Display at $25/hr = at the API dev median globally. Clients read the profile, not filtered out by $45 with zero reviews

- Fixed-price is primary play — display rate is a door-opener; real premium comes from speed advantage on scoped projects
- First-contract urgency: 67% dropout rate beyond 90 days. Every week without a contract compounds the risk

### Hourly Engagement Strategy

**Starting range**: $25–30/hr (display $25, propose $30 if they engage)
**Bangladesh baseline**: $20-30/hr. You enter at the competitive peak of that range.
**Absolute floor**: $20/hr (only for the very first contract, only hourly). Below that, after Upwork's 20% fee on first $500, you net $16/hr — below CLAUDE.md floor.
**Preferred minimum**: $25/hr hourly. At $25, after fee, you net $20/hr — sustainable.
**First-contract logic**: Your BDT 34,000/month target (~$279 USD) requires only 14 hours/month at $20 net. Real target is building review base that unlocks $45-70/hr within 2-4 months (typically after 3-5 contracts).

### Fixed-Price Strategy (Leverage Speed Advantage)

**Why**: Irfan's demonstrated velocity (771 lines/hr, 10.7 tests/hr, 53-107x marketplace average) means you quote fixed-price and **keep the speed premium**.

**Formula**:

- Estimate task in "ideal hours" (e.g., API integration = 12 ideal hours)
- Apply "reality multiplier" (1.5x-2x for unknowns): 12 x 1.5 = 18 billed hours
- Quote as fixed price: 18 hrs x $30/hr = **$540 fixed**, deliver in 5-7 calendar days
- **Actual effort**: ~7 hours (with your speed advantage)
- **Effective rate**: $540 / 7 actual hours = **$77/hr**
- After first 3 reviews, raise multiplier to $40-45/hr: same scope becomes $720-810 fixed

**Client win**: Scope + timeline clarity. No surprise bills.
**Irfan win**: Speed premium without scope creep.

**Fixed-Price Tiers** (to add to Project Catalog):

1. **Starter** ($250-300): Script, small automation, data pipeline
2. **Standard** ($500-800): API integration, webhook setup, third-party connectivity
3. **Deep Integration** ($1500-2000): Multi-service orchestration, error handling, observability

Note: These tiers are priced to be competitive for a zero-review profile while still yielding $30-60/hr effective rate at your speed. Raise tiers by 30-50% after first 3 positive reviews.

## SECTION 5: ITERATIVE CALIBRATION CYCLE (4 Weeks)

### Calibration Framework

The goal is not "100% profile completion + first contract within 30 days" as a monolithic target. Instead: **four-week feedback loop where you measure, tweak, and evaluate weekly**. Each week you ask: _"Am I finding better and more relevant audience in the stream than last week who are more in scope of my listings and likely to take action?"_

---

### Week 1: Foundation + Signal Setup (March 16–22)

**Objective**: Profile complete + baseline metrics established

**What to do**:

- Complete all profile fields (headline, bio, employment history, rate set to $25/hr)
- Upload video intro; submit ID verification
- Buy initial Connects batch (30-40 connects, ~$15-20)
- Take profile screenshots before/after

**Measurable signal**: Profile completeness badge at 100%

**Thing to tweak**: None yet — establish baseline

**Evaluation question**: Is the profile 100% complete and visually coherent?

---

### Week 2: First Wave + Response Analysis (March 23–29)

**Objective**: Send targeted proposals; track which job types get responses

**What to do**:

- Filter 8-10 high-fit jobs (API integration, <7 days old, <$2k budget)
- Draft 8-10 tailored proposals (not generic)
- Submit all proposals; monitor responses daily
- Log response type: interest, negotiation, no-response, low-ball offer

**Measurable signal**: # of responses received vs. proposals sent (target: 2-4 responses from 10 proposals = 20-40% hit rate)

**Thing to tweak**: If hit rate <20%, adjust job filtering criteria (expand to Python automation or webhook setup). If responses are all low-ball, keep rate at $25 (don't lower) — you're hitting the right audience.

**Evaluation question**: Which job types and client profiles are responding? Are they in scope (API integration focus) or drifting to generic Python work?

---

### Week 3: Targeting Refinement + Rate Signal (March 30–April 5)

**Objective**: Double down on what converted; adjust rate if data supports it

**What to do**:

- Send 5-8 more proposals, biased toward job types that got responses Week 2
- If you have 1-2 promising leads in negotiation, lock down scope and timeline
- Monitor: are negotiated rates tracking to $25-30/hr or lower?
- Document: which portfolio items get mentioned in client responses?

**Measurable signal**: Contract signed or in final negotiation (target: 1 contract by end of Week 3)

**Thing to tweak**:

- If you've got 1+ contract signed: stick with $25 display rate (mission accomplished on speed-to-first-contract)
- If still searching: expand daily outreach to 3-4 proposals/day; loosen niche slightly to include Python automation
- If getting responses but no close: adjust proposal opening to be more direct on scope/timeline

**Evaluation question**: Are you landing contracts in your core niche (API integration + resilience), or trending toward generic Python work? Does the client audience signal quality (serious scope, fair budgets)?

A: Likely causes: (1) proposals too generic, (2) targeting wrong job types, (3) Connects running out. Mitigation: expand daily searches to 5-6 jobs/day, broaden niche to include Python automation and webhook setup, buy additional Connects, keep rate at $25/hr (don't lower).

**Q: Should I accept work below $25/hr to land a first contract?**
A: Only if it's a strong portfolio piece and takes <5 hours. Floor: $20/hr hourly. For fixed-price, floor is effective $30/hr (don't take a $50 job that takes 3+ hours). Once you have a 5-star review, raise display to $35/hr immediately.

**Q: What if the first contract scope creeps?**
A: Set a boundary: "Scope includes [X, Y, Z]. Additional features require a separate contract. I can add that in a new proposal." Document scope in the contract description upfront.

**Q: Won't $25/hr make me look cheap?**
A: No. $25 is the global API developer median on Upwork. Combined with your portfolio (published PyPI library, 190k LOC framework, US education), it signals "competitive specialist," not "cheap labor." The portfolio does the selling. The rate opens the door. Fixed-price contracts are where your speed premium materializes — clients never see your hourly rate on those.

**Q: How do I handle a client asking for a lower rate in negotiation?**
A: Response: "My rate reflects the quality and speed of delivery. I'm happy to discuss scope—if we narrow features, I can reduce timeline and cost per milestone. What's most important for your timeline?"

**Q: Will my video intro hurt me if I have an accent?**
A: No. Clear English + confidence > perfect accent. Speak slowly, pause between thoughts, make eye contact with camera.

**Q: What if I close the first contract early (before Week 3)?**
A: Excellent. Shift focus from outreach to execution. Continue daily standups with client. You still target Week 4 for 5-star review + Rising Talent badge. Use the early win to refine fixed-price tiers (raise by 20-30%) and plan the next contract push once this one is delivered.
