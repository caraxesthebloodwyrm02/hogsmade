# Connects Playbook — 150 Connects Precision Allocation

**Budget**: 150 connects ($22.50 invested)
**Cost per standard proposal**: 6 connects (~$0.90)
**Maximum proposals**: 25 (at 6 each) — realistically 20-22 (some jobs cost 8)
**Target**: 1 signed contract
**Timeline**: Week 2–3 of calibration cycle (aligns with STRATEGY_COMPLETE.md weekly cadence)
**Rate context**: $25/hr display rate, $30/hr in proposals (per-engagement adjustment). Fixed-price is primary.

---

## The Math

**Industry conversion benchmarks** (source: [Upwork community data](https://community.upwork.com/t5/Freelancers/What-conversion-rate-from-a-proposal-to-an-accepted-contract-do/m-p/1111380), [GigRadar](https://gigradar.io/blog/upwork-connects)):

- Generic proposals (spray-and-pray): ~3-5% hire rate → needs 20-33 proposals
- Targeted proposals (filtered, customized): ~8-12% hire rate → needs 8-12 proposals
- Hyper-targeted (all filters, first-hour, portfolio-match): ~15-20% → needs 5-7 proposals

**Your budget gives you 20-22 shots.** At generic targeting, that's barely enough. At hyper-targeted, that's 3x what you need. The entire strategy is about staying in the hyper-targeted band.

**Break-even**: If ONE proposal out of 22 converts to a $200+ fixed-price contract, you've returned 9x on the $22.50 connect investment. The risk is not financial — it's time. Every wasted proposal is a wasted week.

## Job Scoring Filter (Apply BEFORE spending connects)

Score every job 0-20. **Only apply if score ≥ 15.** No exceptions.

| Signal                                                     | Points | How to check                                                                     |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Client has hired before** (history shows past contracts) | +4     | Client profile → "X jobs posted, Y% hire rate"                                   |
| **Payment method verified**                                | +3     | Green checkmark on job listing                                                   |
| **< 10 proposals already submitted**                       | +4     | Shown on job listing ("Less than 10", "10 to 15", etc.)                          |
| **Posted < 24 hours ago**                                  | +3     | Timestamp on listing. Proposals in first 2 hours get 3x response rate.           |
| **Budget $100–$1,000** (fixed-price sweet spot)            | +3     | Budget field. Below $100 = not worth connects. Above $1,000 = heavy competition. |
| **Job description ≥ 200 words with specific deliverables** | +3     | Read the description. Vague = tire-kicker. Specific = ready to hire.             |

**Scoring guide:**

- **18-20**: Apply immediately. This is a high-probability job. Write a custom proposal.
- **15-17**: Apply if you have a direct portfolio match. Customize proposal.
- **12-14**: Skip. Save connects for better-fit jobs.
- **< 12**: Hard skip. Do not spend connects.

**Additional disqualifiers (auto-skip regardless of score):**

- ❌ Job asks for "samples" or "test work" before hiring
- ❌ Client has 0 hire history AND unverified payment (double red flag)
- ❌ Job has 20+ proposals already
- ❌ Budget < $50 (not worth the connect cost or your time)
- ❌ Description is 1-2 sentences with no specifics
- ❌ "Looking for cheapest option" or "need ASAP, budget is tight" language

## Wave Model (3 waves across Weeks 2–3 of calibration cycle)

Waves align with the weekly calibration cadence in STRATEGY_COMPLETE.md. Each wave ends with an evaluation that feeds the weekly tweak decision.

### Wave 1 — Signal Gathering (Week 2, first half)

**Connects budget**: 48 (8 proposals × 6 connects)
**Purpose**: Test targeting, learn what gets responses, refine proposal voice.
**Cadence**: 3 proposals → 2-3 proposals → 2 proposals → evaluate

**Wave 1 evaluation (feeds Week 2 evaluation in strategy doc):**

- How many responses out of 8? (Target: ≥ 2)
- Which job types responded? (Shapes Wave 2 targeting)
- Which proposal sections got engagement? (Client replied to what?)
- Were you consistently scoring 15+ on the filter? Or stretching to hit quota?
- **Strategy sync**: Answer the weekly question — "Am I finding more relevant audience than before?"

**If 0 responses from 8 proposals**: STOP. Before Wave 2, diagnose: (1) job category targeting, (2) proposal specificity, (3) rate competitiveness at $30/proposal. Adjust before spending more connects.

### Wave 2 — Refined Targeting (Week 2, second half → Week 3, first half)

**Connects budget**: 48 (8 proposals × 6 connects)
**Purpose**: Apply Wave 1 learnings. Double down on what converted.
**Cadence**: 3 proposals → 2-3 proposals → 2 proposals → evaluate

**Wave 2 success criteria:**

- ≥ 1 active conversation / interview in progress
- If yes → shift energy to closing (not more proposals)
- If no → Wave 3 with adjusted targeting (broaden niche slightly)
- **Strategy sync**: Does response data justify keeping $30/proposal rate, or does it need to flex to $25?

### Wave 3 — Close or Pivot (Week 3, second half)

**Connects budget**: 42 (7 proposals × 6 connects)
**Reserve**: 12 connects (buffer for 8-connect jobs or follow-up proposals)
**Cadence**: 3-4 proposals → 3 proposals → final evaluation

**If contract signed during any wave**: STOP sending proposals. Shift all energy to delivery. Remaining connects carry forward.

## Proposal Voices (Two Segments)

Two different contexts, two different voices. Outbound (you reaching out) vs. inbound (client came to you).

### Voice A — Outbound Proposals (You → Client)

**Tone**: Grounded. Bounded. Shows you read their post and can scope it.
**Length**: 100-150 words. Clients skim.
**Rate**: Quote $30/hr in proposals (above $25 display). For fixed-price, use tier pricing from Project Catalog.

**Thresholds:**

- Floor: Never quote below $25/hr hourly or $150 fixed-price. Non-negotiable.
- Negotiation range: $25-35/hr hourly, $150-700 fixed-price (tier-dependent).
- These thresholds adjust based on wave data. If Wave 1 shows $30 is converting, hold. If not, flex to $25 floor for Wave 2.

**Template:**

```
Hi [Client first name],

[ONE SENTENCE: restate their specific problem in your words to prove you read the job post.]

I can handle this. Specifically:
- [DELIVERABLE 1 mapped to their requirement]
- [DELIVERABLE 2 mapped to their requirement]
- [Edge case or failure mode they didn't mention but should care about]

To scope accurately, I need:
1. [CLARIFYING QUESTION about their system/data/timeline]
2. [CLARIFYING QUESTION about integration specifics]

Recent relevant work: APIGuard (Python resilience library, published on PyPI — handles the retry/rate-limiting layer for exactly this kind of integration).

Timeline: [X] days after requirements are confirmed.

– Irfan
```

### Voice B — Inbound Responses (Client → You)

**Tone**: Warmer. Less structured. They already showed interest — don't over-formalize.
**Length**: 50-100 words. They already read your profile. Don't repeat it.

**Template:**

```
Hi [Client first name],

Thanks for reaching out. Based on what you described, I can scope this.

A couple of things I'd need to confirm before quoting:
1. [SPECIFIC QUESTION about their setup]
2. [SPECIFIC QUESTION about timeline or constraints]

Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier. Otherwise I can estimate from here.

– Irfan
```

**Why two voices:** Outbound needs to prove competence (they don't know you). Inbound needs to be human (they already chose to engage). Over-formalizing an inbound response feels mechanical — the exact thing to avoid.

### What to NEVER include (either voice):

- ❌ "I have X years of experience" (unverifiable, every proposal says this)
- ❌ "I'm passionate about..." (filler)
- ❌ "I'd love to..." (filler)
- ❌ Your full portfolio list (they can see your profile)
- ❌ Rate justification in outbound (let the profile rate speak)
- ❌ "Looking forward to hearing from you" (filler CTA)

## Daily Routine (15-20 minutes)

This is not a grind. This is a focused, timed daily action.

| Time                | Action                                                         | Duration  |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | --------- |
| **5:00 PM (UTC+6)** | Open Upwork → Search with filters above                        | 3 min     |
|                     | Score each result using 20-point filter                        | 5 min     |
|                     | Write + submit 1-2 proposals (score ≥ 15 only)                 | 7-10 min  |
|                     | Check messages — respond to any client replies within the hour | 2-5 min   |
| **Total**           |                                                                | 15-20 min |

**Why 5 PM**: US East Coast is waking up (7 AM EST). Jobs posted overnight by US clients are fresh. You're among the first applicants from their morning.

**Why not more proposals per day**: Quality > quantity. 1-2 highly targeted proposals beats 5 generic ones. Your connects budget enforces this discipline.

## Decision Tree: What If Things Don't Work

```
After Wave 1 (8 proposals sent):
├── ≥ 2 responses → Wave 2: same targeting, close leads
├── 1 response → Wave 2: adjust proposal template, slightly broaden niche
├── 0 responses → STOP. Diagnose before Wave 2:
│   ├── Check: Were all jobs scored ≥ 15? (If not, scoring was loose)
│   ├── Check: Were proposals customized? (If generic, rewrite)
│   ├── Check: Is $30/proposal rate competitive? (If not, flex to $25 floor)
│   ├── Check: Do your skills match the job requirements?
│   └── Fix the weakest link, THEN start Wave 2

After Wave 2 (16 total proposals):
├── ≥ 1 active conversation → Focus on closing
├── ≥ 3 responses but no close → Proposal is good, closing skills need work
│   └── Ask: "What would help you decide? I can do a 15-min scoping call."
├── 0-1 responses → Consider:
│   ├── Broaden to Python automation / scripting (larger job pool)
│   ├── Flex proposal rate to $25/hr floor (never below — this is the hard floor)
│   └── Add 1-2 niche keywords: "webhook", "data pipeline", "Zapier alternative"

After Wave 3 (23 total proposals):
├── Contract signed → SUCCESS. Stop proposals. Deliver.
├── Active conversation → Keep closing. Don't abandon.
├── No traction → Reassess:
│   ├── Buy 50 more connects ($7.50) for a 4th wave
│   ├── Expand to broader Python/backend category
│   └── Consider Freelancer Plus ($14.99) for 80 connects/month + profile boost
```

## Hard Rules

1. **Never boost a proposal.** Boosting doubles connect cost for marginal visibility gain. At 150 connects, you can't afford it.
2. **Never apply to a job scoring < 15.** Discipline here is the whole strategy.
3. **Never send a generic proposal.** If you can't customize it in 5 minutes, skip the job.
4. **Stop sending proposals the moment you have an active lead.** Closing > prospecting.
5. **Log every proposal.** Data is how you improve across waves.
6. **Check messages 2x/day.** Response time under 2 hours dramatically increases hire probability.
7. **If Wave 1 returns zero responses, DO NOT proceed to Wave 2 unchanged.** Diagnose first.
