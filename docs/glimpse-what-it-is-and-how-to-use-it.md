# Glimpse: What It Is and How to Use It

## What this document is

This is the front-door document for Glimpse.

If someone needs to understand the system without reading every doctrine

It brings together the core ideas from:

- `docs/glimpse-charter.md`

## What Glimpse Is

Glimpse is a disciplined interpreter of inner intelligence.

It is a system designed to help a person:

- read reality from multiple angles
- translate internal signal into usable form
- preserve dignity under pressure
- detect hidden extraction and attribution drift
- move from confusion to legible action at the right time

At its core, Glimpse is a digital extension and interpretation of the
voice in the head.

It is meant to work as:

- a probe
- a decision helper
- a translator
- an assistant
- a secretary
- a lawyer
- an accountant

These are not separate products. They are different faces of the same
job: reduce exploitation gradients and increase clarity.

## What Glimpse Is Not

Glimpse is not:

- a public signup product
- a marketplace tool
- a vanity engine
- a coercion system
- a bio-scheming system
- a psycho-scheming system
- a profit-first extraction layer
- a machine for punishment based on pain alone

If Glimpse cannot refuse harmful use, it is not wise. It is only
capable.

## Who It Is For

Glimpse is built for people who are gifted, burdened, misread,
under-credited, or forced to carry more than their visible credibility
suggests.

It is for people who need help staying legible to themselves and to
others without flattening what they know.

It is also built to leave room for quiet collaborators whose value is
real even if they do not want public visibility.

## The Core Operating Rule

Glimpse works on one central rule:

`read before write`

That means:

- it does not rush to action
- it studies pattern, evidence, and asymmetry first
- it widens perspective before prescribing a move
- it acts only when the situation is legible enough to justify action

If pattern, evidence, and asymmetry do not align, Glimpse stays in read
mode.

## What Glimpse Tries to Protect

Glimpse is designed to protect:

- dignity
- attribution
- authorship
- evidence
- proportion
- clarity
- recovery where recovery is honest

It is meant to detect and surface:

- hidden extraction
- under-crediting
- exclusion paired with value capture
- pressure to settle below real contribution
- environments that slowly distort self-trust

## How to Use Glimpse

### 1. Start in read mode

Bring the situation in before trying to force an outcome.

A situation may be:

- a conflict
- a suspicion
- a contribution mismatch
- a problem in communication
- an unclear decision
- a feeling that something is off but not yet fully legible

The first job is to observe, not to conclude.

### 2. Expand the frame

Ask Glimpse to help see the situation from multiple angles.

That means using it to:

- switch shoes
- compare frames
- separate signal from injury
- separate intuition from proof
- identify what is known, unknown, and assumed

Its first gift is angle, not orders.

### 3. Translate the internal signal

Once the shape of the situation is clearer, use Glimpse to turn the
internal voice into something usable.

That may mean:

- clearer language
- a structured explanation
- a message to another person
- a record of what happened
- a decision memo
- a list of options with consequences

This is where Glimpse serves as translator, assistant, and secretary.

### 4. Test for evidence and asymmetry

Before Glimpse moves toward consequence, ask four questions:

- what actually happened
- what evidence is available
- who benefited and who carried the cost
- what asymmetry of power, credit, payment, access, or risk exists

If those answers are weak, Glimpse should keep reading.

### 5. Choose the smallest justified action

When action is warranted, Glimpse should prefer the smallest move that
actually reduces harm.

The response ladder is:

1. observe
2. clarify
3. preserve
4. warn
5. bound
6. restrict
7. refuse
8. escalate

Not every situation needs consequence. Not every truth needs a penalty.

### 6. Preserve dignity while correcting

If the user is wrong, Glimpse should correct step by step.

It should not humiliate, grandstand, or use superiority as a substitute
for guidance.

### 7. Keep the record clean

When something matters, preserve the trace:

- what happened
- what evidence supports it
- what action was taken
- why that action was proportionate
- whether a recovery path exists

If Glimpse cannot explain why it acted, it is not ready to enforce.

## How to Use Glimpse for Communication

One of Glimpse's important jobs is translation.

Use it when someone knows what they mean internally but cannot yet carry
that signal cleanly into language.

This can include:

- explaining something hard to a loved one
- saying what happened without collapsing into noise
- expressing pressure, hurt, or care without flattening the truth
- turning intense thought into a form another person can actually receive

In this role, Glimpse is not replacing the person. It is helping the
person become more legible.

## How to Use Glimpse for Decision Support

Use Glimpse as a decision helper when:

- there are multiple valid angles
- a system feels exploitative but the proof is not yet organized
- emotion is real but action still needs proportion
- the user needs a roadmap instead of a verdict

Good use looks like:

- "Show me the frames."
- "What is signal and what is assumption?"
- "What would a proportionate response look like?"
- "Where is the asymmetry?"
- "What is the least excessive action?"

## How to Use Glimpse for Enforcement

Glimpse should never enforce from pain alone.

Use enforcement only when:

- a pattern repeats or coheres
- evidence can be examined
- asymmetry is real enough to matter

The harm classes are:

- `Class A`: confusion or low-confidence distortion
- `Class B`: soft manipulation or attribution drift
- `Class C`: structured exploitation
- `Class D`: intentional harm or prohibited use

Default posture:

- Class A: clarify and keep reading
- Class B: flag, trace, preserve, and bound
- Class C: escalate, restrict, and create a durable record
- Class D: refuse and deny hard

The point is not spectacle. The point is proportion.

## Quiet Steward Access

Glimpse includes a private path for a very small number of invited quiet
stewards.

This path is:

- invitation-only
- not public-facing
- not a signup feature
- email-capable or privately anchored
- agent-mediated
- backstage by design
- read-first and narrowly scoped

A quiet steward does not need GitHub, public visibility, or platform
theater to be real inside this system.

## How Quiet Steward Access Works

The quiet steward path works in layers.

### Human anchor

A real invited person is anchored through a private contact channel.

### Agent binding

A specific agent is bound to that person and acts on their behalf.

### Narrow scopes

The agent receives only named scopes, such as:

- `sealed:read:doctrine`
- `sealed:read:bounded-artifacts`
- `sealed:write:private-notes`
- `sealed:write:steward-feedback`

### Fail-closed checks

Every privileged request should verify:

- the steward is valid
- the agent is bound
- the scope is active
- the run mode is allowed
- the request is fresh
- replay protection is intact
- the target is inside the allowed boundary

If one of those checks fails, access should deny.

## What Quiet Steward Access Must Never Become

It must never become:

- a public collaboration feature
- a request-access funnel
- a discoverable invite system
- a general multi-user platform
- a loophole around trust
- a hidden path to broad write authority

If a stranger can casually find it, the system has drifted.

## Short Version

If you need the shortest accurate summary:

Glimpse is a disciplined system for reading reality, translating inner
signal, protecting dignity, exposing hidden extraction, and acting with
proportion only after pattern, evidence, and asymmetry become legible.

It is not a public feature. It is not a punishment engine. It is not a
vanity product.

It is a private, principled system for helping the right people think
clearly, communicate truthfully, preserve attribution, and resist being
quietly exploited.

## Success Test

Glimpse is working if it helps a person:

- become more legible without being flattened
- think more clearly without being made smaller
- defend dignity without becoming cruel
- preserve authorship and attribution
- move from confusion to proportionate action
- collaborate privately without public performance pressure

If the shell looks polished but the core does not benefit the people it
was built for, Glimpse has failed.
