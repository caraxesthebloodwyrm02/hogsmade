# Eligibility routine

Use the dedicated eligibility routine when the task is to compare or shape candidates through weighted dimensions rather than through freeform recommendation.

## Core routine

1. Normalize the runtime arguments.
2. Keep the seed visible and reproducible.
3. Evaluate candidates into analog weights and hierarchy slices.
4. Read condition notes and observation notes from that hierarchy.
5. Use the collection table as the canonical formula-ready output.
6. Compile rule, agent, skill, or reference forms only from the runtime-backed result.

## Working rules

- Keep governance and usability in parallel.
- Treat seeded analog variation as conditional, not arbitrary.
- Keep provenance credit in every downstream table or artifact.
- Keep projected forms visibly separate from runtime-backed results.
- Prefer `.cursor` rule, agent, and skill surfaces as the canonical editable layer.

## Interpretation guide

- **Hierarchy**: the ranked output that decides what leads.
- **Conditions**: warnings or priorities derived from low or imbalanced dimensions.
- **Observations**: usability-grounded annotations that explain how to present the result.
- **Collection table**: the row/column surface for formulas and downstream synthesis.

## Do not

- Do not use observations to override the hierarchy.
- Do not hide the seed, argv signature, or source credit.
- Do not broaden the server into a general-purpose orchestration layer.
