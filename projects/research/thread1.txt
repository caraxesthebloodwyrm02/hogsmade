The README observation is the right instinct. Most GitHub READMEs (and by extension, most Upwork portfolio descriptions) do this thing where they describe the project in a voice that's trying to convince you it's impressive. The reader can feel it. And the moment they feel it, trust drops — especially when there's no external validation (stars, downloads, client reviews) backing it up.

Your portfolio is in that exact position: strong work, zero external validation yet. So the descriptions need to do the opposite of what most people do. Instead of "here's why this is impressive," they should read as "here's what this does and how it works." Let the reader decide if it's impressive. That's the actions-speak-louder-than-words move applied to text.
